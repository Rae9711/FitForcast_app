## User Stories

### Jordan (28, Active Gym-Goer)

**Profile**
- Strength training enthusiast, 4–5 days/week  
- Wants to understand **energy recovery after strength workouts**  
- Tracks nutrition loosely but consistently  

**Typical Day Pattern**
- **Morning (6–7am)** – 60–90 min strength session (bench, squats, deadlifts)  
  - Pre-feeling: Energy 2/5, Valence 3/5, Stress 3/5  
  - Post-feeling: Energy 4–5/5, Valence 5/5, Stress 1/5  
- **Lunch (12pm)** – "Chicken breast, brown rice, broccoli"  
  - Post-feeling: Energy 4/5, Valence 4/5, Stress 2/5  
- **Evening** – Rest day or 20–30 min walk  

**Expected MVP Insights (after ~20–30 entries)**
- ✓ Energy uplift after strength workouts  
- ✓ Routine days stabilize mood  
- ✓ Consistent strength training frequency  

---

### Alex (35, Fitness & Nutrition Conscious)

**Profile**
- Recently adopted stricter **nutrition + workout routine**  
- Highly sensitive to **meal timing** effects on sleep and morning energy  
- Logs consistently but sometimes late at night  

**Typical Day Pattern**
- **Breakfast (7am)** – "Eggs, toast, coffee"  
  - Post-feeling: Energy 4/5, Valence 4/5, Stress 3/5  
- **Lunch (12:30pm)** – Salad or leftovers  
- **Workout (5–6pm)** – 30 min moderate run or 45 min yoga  
- **Dinner (6–9:30pm)** – Occasionally very late after social events  
  - Next morning energy:  
    - 3–4/5 if dinner was late (after 9pm)  
    - 4–5/5 if dinner was early (before 7:30pm)  

**Expected MVP Insights (after ~25–30 entries)**
- ✓ Late meals correlate with lower morning energy  
- ✓ Consistent breakfast timing improves mood stability  
- ✗ Energy uplift after strength (mostly cardio/yoga)  

---

### Sam (42, New to Structured Logging)

**Profile**
- Returning to fitness after a long break (5+ years)  
- Initially inconsistent logger; becomes more structured over time  
- Highly influenced by **early feedback** from insights  

**Logging Timeline**
- **Days 1–10** – Sparse: 1–2 entries/day, minimal feelings  
  - Expected: No insights yet; baselines exist but low-confidence  
- **Days 11–20** – 3–4 entries/day, detailed inputs with feelings  
  - Expected: First insights fire once min data points reached  
- **Days 21+** – Consistent, detailed logging  
  - Expected: All relevant insights (energy, late meals, routine mood) can fire  

**Expected MVP Behaviors**
- UI communicates "more data needed" clearly in early days  
- No premature insights on very sparse data  
- Insights become more specific and stable as logging density grows
